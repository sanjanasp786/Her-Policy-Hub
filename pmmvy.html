Pradha Mantri Matru Vandana Yojna

What is it?

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY) is a maternity benefit program launched by the Government of India on 1st January 2017. The scheme aims to provide financial assistance to pregnant and lactating women to ensure proper nutrition and health care during their pregnancy and after childbirth.

Benifits

• Provides financial assistance to pregnant and lactating women to meet their nutritional and health care needs. • Promotes early registration of pregnancy and antenatal care, which is crucial for maternal and child health. • Encourages institutional delivery and vaccination of children, leading to improved health outcomes. • Empowers women by providing them with financial support during a critical phase of their lives.

Eligibility

• The scheme is applicable to pregnant women and lactating mothers. •The woman must be aged 19 years or above. •The woman must be pregnant for the first time. •The benefits under the scheme can be availed for the first live birth. •The woman must register herself at the Anganwadi center or with the Accredited Social Health Activist (ASHA) of her area within the first four months of pregnancy.

Statistics

• PMMVY has benefited millions of pregnant and lactating women across India, providing them with much-needed financial assistance and support. •The scheme has contributed to improving maternal and child health outcomes, including increased institutional deliveries and vaccination coverage.
