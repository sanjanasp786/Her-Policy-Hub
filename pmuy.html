Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojna

What is it?

Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) is a flagship social welfare scheme of the Government of India launched on 1 May 2016. The scheme aims to provide clean cooking fuel (LPG - Liquefied Petroleum Gas) to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households. The primary objective is to replace unclean cooking fuels such as kerosene with LPG, thereby improving the health and environment of women and their families.

Benifits

• Provides a financial support of Rs 1,600 to eligible BPL households for getting a new LPG connection. • Facilitates EMI facility for purchasing gas stove and refilling cost. • Ensures easy access to LPG refills through an extensive network of distributors.

Eligibility

• The applicant should be a woman above the age of 18 years. • The household should be classified as Below Poverty Line (BPL). •The applicant should not already own an LPG connection in her name

Statistics

• Over 8.75 crore LPG connections have been released under PMUY since its inception. •The scheme has covered almost all districts across the country, with a significant focus on rural and remote areas. •The scheme has led to a substantial increase in LPG penetration in rural areas, improving the lives of millions of women by providing them with a clean cooking fuel option.
